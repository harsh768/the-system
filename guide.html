What it is

The System is a gamified habit & goal tracker. You define what you want to become; it turns that into daily quests. Completing them earns XP (levels you up, rank E→S), Gold (spend on real rewards you choose), builds streaks, raises seven attributes, and unlocks achievements. Everything is general and fully editable — fitness, study, work, self-care, money, or anything you invent.

Private by design: all your data lives on your device. No account, no servers, works offline.

Get started in 3 steps

1
Onboard. Enter your name, pick the areas you care about (Fitness, Nutrition, Mind & Skill, Work & Focus, Self-care, Money), choose starter quests, and pick 2–3 keystones.
2
Each day, open Home and tick quests as you do them. You instantly earn XP + Gold. Tap a quest's text for details.
3
Clear your keystones (the 🔥 ones) every day to keep your streak alive — even on a busy day, just hit those.

How the game works

Quests

Build your own (title, attribute, difficulty → sets XP/gold, repeat days) or add from the preset library. Keep them specific and doable. Harder quests pay more gold, so rewards are genuinely earned.

Keystones & streaks

Mark 2–3 quests as keystones (⭐). Only these protect your streak — clear them daily and the chain survives even if you skip smaller stuff. Clearing them gives a daily bonus that grows with your streak length, with extra celebrations at 7/14/30/50/100 days.

Attributes

Every quest trains one of seven attributes — STR (training), VIT (food/sleep), AGI (cardio/movement), INT (study/skills), Focus (work), SEN (self-care), WLT (money). The radar shows your 14-day consistency in each — aim for a balanced shape.

Achievements & weekly challenge

15 badges unlock as you hit milestones (first quest, streaks, levels, 100/500 quests, etc.), each granting bonus gold. A fresh weekly challenge (clear N quests) rewards XP + gold when you complete it.

Rewards & money

Define real rewards (a treat, a movie, a purchase) and buy them with gold. The optional money manager keeps your spending honest — set a savings goal and budget; overspending eats into projected savings.

Tips

  • Start small — 5–8 quests is plenty. Add more once it's a habit.
  • Consistency > intensity. A tiny quest done daily beats a big one done rarely.
  • Spend your gold. Earned rewards keep you motivated.
  • Write one daily note. Over months it becomes proof of how far you've come.
  • Back up before switching phones: Settings → Export Backup.

For the owner — Publishing step by step

This section is for you (the developer). It's safe to leave it in — users just see a guide. Here's the full path from "it's live as a web app" to "it's on the stores," with what's already built.

Already done ✅ — the app is a complete, installable PWA, live at harsh768.github.io/the-system/: manifest, icons, offline service worker with auto-update, privacy policy page, and all features above. It's architected as clean static web so it wraps cleanly for both stores.

Phase 1 — Use & share now (free)

1
Open the live URL on a phone. iPhone: Safari → Share → Add to Home Screen. Android: Chrome → Install app. It runs fullscreen, offline, and updates itself.
2
Share the link with anyone — it works for everyone immediately, no store needed.

Phase 2 — Google Play (easiest, ~$25 one-time)

1
Create a Google Play Console account (one-time $25 fee). Complete identity verification and sign the agreements.
2
Generate the Android app: go to PWABuilder.com → paste https://harsh768.github.io/the-system/ → Package For Stores → Android → download the .aab file and the assetlinks.json it generates.
3
Verify domain ownership: put that assetlinks.json in the repo at /.well-known/assetlinks.json so the app and website are linked (removes the browser address bar in the installed app).
4
Create the app in Play Console → upload the .aab → fill the store listing (title, short & full description), upload screenshots (take from your phone) + a feature graphic, set content rating, and complete the Data safety form (answer: no data collected — true). Add the privacy policy URL: harsh768.github.io/the-system/privacy.html.
5
Submit for review (usually 1–3 days). Once approved, it's live on Google Play.

Phase 3 — Apple App Store ($99/year)

1
Enroll in the Apple Developer Program ($99/year) and install Xcode (free, on your Mac).
2
Wrap with Capacitor. Capacitor packages the web app into a native iOS project and lets us add the native features Apple wants (push reminders, haptics) so it passes review's "minimum functionality" rule.
3
Build & upload: open the project in Xcode, set the app icon, bundle ID and signing, then Archive → upload to App Store Connect.
4
Create the listing in App Store Connect (description, screenshots, privacy answers) and submit for review.
Why iOS is second: Apple is stricter and pricier than Google, and needs those native touches. Do Android first to get live + gather reviews, then iOS.

Phase 4 — Monetization (free at launch)

1
Launch 100% free to grow users and ratings.
2
Later, add Premium via RevenueCat (free tier; handles Apple + Google billing). Premium can unlock cloud backup/sync, extra themes, premium templates, and widgets. Core stays free forever.
3
For cloud sync (so users don't lose data when switching phones), add a small backend like Supabase (free tier) — also a great premium hook.
